# Why Do We Need Each Other?

## Children's Book: The Air in the Room

### 1. The Glass

Mina placed a glass of water on the teacher's desk.

"Who needs this?" she asked.

"Me," Aksel said.

"Everyone," Iben said.

"Plants," Nora said.

"Fish," Samir said.

"Fish do not live in glasses," Aksel said.

"You know what I mean," Samir said.

Mina nodded.

"Today we examine why we need each other."

"Because nobody knows everything," Maja said.

"Because people get lonely," Iben said.

"Because someone has to clean up after other people," Samir said, looking at Aksel.

"What is that supposed to mean?" Aksel asked.

Then the lights went out.

Only for four seconds.

But four seconds is long enough for a room to remember it does not control everything.

When the lights came back, the glass had fallen.

Water ran across the desk.

"Who did it?" Aksel asked.

Mina looked at the water.

"That is not always the first question."

"What is the first question?"

"What do we need now?"

### 2. Before Blame

They found paper towels.

Maja lifted the books.

Samir dried the floor.

Iben moved the projector cable.

Aksel stood still at first.

Then he took the mop.

"I did not do it," he said.

"Maybe not," Mina said. "But the water is still here."

That was annoyingly true.

When the floor was dry, Mina placed the Council cards on the table.

Christianity asked: **Who gets left behind if everyone only saves their own things?**

Islam asked: **What do we owe the community when something goes wrong?**

Buddhism asked: **What fear makes us hard?**

Plato asked: **Do we see the person, or only the blame?**

Kant asked: **Do we treat each other as people, or as tools?**

Science asked: **What do we actually know?**

Psychology asked: **Are we calm enough to help before we accuse?**

The Power Critic asked: **Who avoids responsibility because others always clean?**

The Peacebuilder asked: **What is the first repairing action?**

The Child asked:

**Why do people become so busy finding who did it that they forget who needs help?**

Aksel leaned back.

"You do need to know who did it."

"Sometimes," Mina said. "But not always first."

### 3. Breathing

Nora raised her hand.

"We do not only need each other."

"What do you mean?" Maja asked.

Nora pointed out the window.

The birch tree stood by the gate.

"We need that too."

Aksel sighed.

"Now everything is about trees again."

Mina brought out a green card.

It was The Living World.

It asked:

**What life-support are you damaging for the future in order to comfort your fear now?**

"That is a long question," Iben said.

"Yes," Mina said. "Some questions become long because we made them too small."

The Living World spoke:

Before you argue, you breathe.

Before you plan, you drink.

Before you make rules, you stand on floors made from trees, stone, work, and time.

You are not floating heads.

You are bodies in a living world.

Samir looked at the dark edge of the wet stain.

"So we need water before we can talk about water."

"Yes," Mina said.

"And air before we can disagree," Iben said.

"Yes."

Aksel breathed loudly as a joke.

Then everyone heard their own breathing.

It became strange.

Strange and quiet.

### 4. Invisible Work

After lunch, Lidia came in.

She was the cleaner.

She often smiled, but today she looked tired.

On the floor were sand, milk cartons, paper bits, a broken pencil, and one sock without an owner.

"Hello," Lidia said.

"Hello," the class said.

She began to clean.

The Power Critic's question was still on the board:

**Who avoids responsibility because others always clean?**

Maja stood up.

Then Iben.

Then Samir.

At last, Aksel.

"We can do it," he said.

Lidia looked at him.

"All of it?"

Aksel looked at the floor.

"At least what is ours."

It was a small answer.

But it was better than a large answer that was not true.

They cleaned.

Not like heroes.

Just like people who had finally seen something.

Afterward Nora said:

"We need Lidia."

"Yes," Samir said. "But not so we can stop caring."

Mina wrote on the board:

**Needing someone is not the same as using someone.**

Nobody spoke for a while.

### 5. The Door

Later that week, the classroom door got stuck.

It was not dangerous.

But they could not get out at first.

Aksel pulled.

Samir pushed.

Maja went to get Mina.

Iben calmed a younger child in the hallway.

Nora noticed the window was open and the air pressure made the door heavier.

"Close the window," she said.

They did.

The door opened.

Everyone looked at Nora.

"What?" she said.

"You saved us," Aksel said.

"No," Nora said. "I saw the window."

The Cosmos Minister might have said:

Sometimes help is not being strongest.

Sometimes help is noticing what others miss.

The Child might have asked:

**How many people think they are alone because nobody has noticed the help they carry?**

### Reflection Questions

1. Why did Aksel want to know who was guilty first?
2. When is helping more urgent than blaming?
3. Who does invisible work around you?
4. What is the difference between needing someone and using someone?
5. What do you depend on that you often treat as obvious?
