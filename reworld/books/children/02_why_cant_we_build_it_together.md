# Why Can't We Build It Together?

## Children's Book: The Open Hut

### 1. The Woodpile

Behind the gym there was a pile of wood.

It smelled of rain, dust, and something that might become something.

Mina pointed to it.

"Your class will build a place together."

"A hut," Iben said.

"A stage," Samir said.

"A fort," Aksel said.

"A place where you can be quiet," Nora said.

Everyone looked at her.

Nora did not often speak first.

Maybe that was why nobody heard her properly.

Aksel took the largest pencil.

"We need a leader."

"Why you?" Samir asked.

"Because I get things done."

"Sometimes you only get your things done," Iben said.

Aksel drew a tower.

Samir drew a wall that could open.

Maja drew a ramp.

Nora drew a small corner with a cushion.

When the drawings lay beside each other, they did not look like one hut.

They looked like four wishes arguing quietly.

"We cannot build everything," Aksel said.

"No," Mina said. "But perhaps we can examine what each wish is trying to protect."

### 2. Holding the Hammer

They began without examining.

It went badly.

Aksel carried the most wood.

Maja held the tape measure.

Iben sorted nails.

Samir stood with his drawing in his hand.

Nora waited.

"We are helping," Maja said.

"Yes," Samir said. "But who gets to shape it?"

The word shape stayed in the air.

Helping could mean carrying someone else's plan.

Shaping meant leaving a real mark.

Mina placed the ReWorld Council cards on a plank.

Christianity asked: **Who will have room in what you build?**

Islam asked: **What is fair for the whole group, not only the loudest voice?**

Buddhism asked: **What are you holding so tightly that others cannot enter?**

Plato asked: **Do you see the hut, or only the shadow of your own idea?**

Kant asked: **Could your rule be used by everyone who builds?**

Science asked: **What do you actually have enough material for?**

Psychology asked: **Who becomes tense from having to fight to be heard?**

The Power Critic asked: **Who defines the word together?**

The Peacebuilder asked: **What can you build first without locking anyone out?**

The Child asked:

**Why do you say together when some people only get to hold the hammer after the plan is decided?**

Aksel looked at the hammer in his hand.

Then he put it down.

Not far away.

But down.

### 3. The Old Voice

The Cosmos Minister card lay in the center.

Mina read:

**Call another voice when the question needs more than the room already sees.**

"Who do we need?" Iben asked.

Nora answered before anyone else.

"Someone who remembers."

Mina brought out The Ancestor.

The card looked like old stairs.

The Ancestor asked:

**Humans have tried this before. What have we forgotten?**

Mina read slowly.

"People have built houses, cities, schools, bridges, and nations. Often they said: This is for everyone. Sometimes everyone only meant the people invited to draw the plan."

Samir looked at his opening wall.

"So a hut can look nice and still be wrong?"

"Yes," Mina said. "Not because it is ugly. Because it lies a little."

"Can a hut lie?" Maja asked.

The Child card rested quietly.

Iben answered:

"Maybe if it says welcome but has no room."

### 4. Leaving Space

Science helped them count.

They did not have enough wood for four walls, a roof, a ramp, a stage, a tower, and a quiet corner.

"Then something must go," Aksel said.

"Or something must stay open," Nora said.

This time they heard her.

"An open hut," Samir said.

"With one wall that can move," Iben said.

"A floor that can become a stage," Maja said.

"And a quiet corner," Nora said.

Everyone looked at Aksel.

He looked at his tower.

"No fort?"

"Maybe not now," Mina said.

Aksel breathed out.

"Can we make a small lookout?"

They could.

Not a tower that ruled over everything.

Only a place where someone could see a little farther.

It was not everything Aksel wanted.

It was not everything Samir wanted.

It was not everything Nora wanted.

But it was not nobody's wish either.

### 5. The Unfinished Side

They built for three days.

It became crooked.

Noah measured wrong and got angry at the tape measure.

Maja got paint on her trousers.

Aksel held the hammer too long.

Samir spoke too sharply.

Nora walked away for a while because everyone talked at once.

When she came back, they had saved her quiet corner.

Not because it was decoration.

Because a place to be quiet can also belong to a community.

On the open wall they painted:

**Why can't we build it together?**

Under it, The Child's question:

**Who do we mean when we say we?**

The Cosmos Minister gave no prize.

The Cosmos Minister gathered the questions:

What became better because more people shaped it?

What became harder?

Who was still heard too little?

What must the next class be allowed to change?

Aksel looked at the open side.

"It does not look finished."

"No," Nora said.

"I kind of do not like that."

"Me too," she said.

Then they smiled.

Not because everything was solved.

Because the hut did not pretend.

### Reflection Questions

1. What is the difference between helping and shaping?
2. Who had the most power at the beginning?
3. Why did the Council need The Ancestor?
4. Can something look beautiful and still leave someone out?
5. What would you leave open so someone else could continue?
