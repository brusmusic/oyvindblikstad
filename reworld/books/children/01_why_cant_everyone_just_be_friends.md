# Why Can't Everyone Just Be Friends?

## Children's Book: The Line That Moved

### 1. The Line

There was a line in the schoolyard.

It was not painted. It was only a crack in the asphalt, running from the basketball hoop to the tree that leaned toward the fence.

But during recess, the crack became something else.

On one side were the children who played.

On the other side were the children who waited to be asked.

Iben had noticed it for weeks.

She had also noticed that the line moved.

When Aksel wanted someone on his team, the line almost disappeared.

When Samir asked to join, the line suddenly became wide.

"Teams are full," Aksel said.

"There are only four of you," Samir said.

"That is enough."

Maja looked at the ground. She liked Samir. She also liked being chosen by Aksel.

It is possible to like someone and like your place even more.

Iben felt it in her stomach.

She wanted to say something.

But she knew a line could move again.

It could move around her.

Then Mina came.

Mina was the teacher who did not shout first.

That made her harder to escape.

"What is happening here?" she asked.

"Nothing," Aksel said.

"Then we can examine nothing," Mina said.

No one laughed.

### 2. The Council Under the Stairs

Mina took them to the small space under the library stairs.

There was a round bench and a plant that always looked as if it was trying.

Mina brought out a box.

On the lid it said:

**ReWorld Council**

"Is it a game?" Maja asked.

"Not exactly," Mina said. "It is a room for questions."

"We are not in a room," Aksel said.

Mina looked at him.

"Some rooms are not made of walls."

She placed cards on the bench.

Christianity asked: **Who is being made smallest here?**

Islam asked: **What do we owe the whole group, not only our friends?**

Buddhism asked: **What fear are we holding so tightly that it hurts someone?**

Plato asked: **Do we see the person, or only the role we gave them?**

Kant asked: **Could your rule be used by everyone?**

Science asked: **What do we actually know?**

Psychology asked: **Are our bodies calm enough to think?**

The Power Critic asked: **Who benefits when nobody speaks?**

The Peacebuilder asked: **What must stop before anything can heal?**

Then Mina placed the smallest card in the middle.

**The Child**

It asked:

**Why must someone become small for someone else to feel big?**

Aksel did not laugh.

It was a simple question.

That was why it was difficult.

### 3. Not Just Nice

"I only want to play," Aksel said.

"That may be true," Mina said.

"So I am not mean."

"That may also be true."

Aksel frowned.

Mina placed The Cosmos Minister card beside The Child.

It said:

**Separate harm from shame. Do not use conversation to delay protection. Do not use the claim of truth as a knife.**

"What does that mean?" Maja asked.

"It means we do not turn Aksel into a monster," Mina said. "And we do not pretend Samir is not being kept out."

Samir stared at the floor.

"I do not want to be a problem," he said.

"You are not the problem," Iben said.

She said it quietly.

But she said it.

The line moved a little.

"What do we actually know?" Mina asked.

"Samir asked," Maja said.

"We said no," Aksel said.

"You often say no," Samir said.

"I have seen it," Iben said.

That hurt most.

Not because Iben had done the worst thing.

But because she had known enough not to be innocent.

### 4. The First Thing

"Before we make rules," Mina said, "we need to ask what must stop."

"Saying the teams are full when they are not," Samir said.

"Laughing when someone asks," Iben said.

"Pretending it is only about football," Maja said.

Aksel looked at his shoes.

"Deciding everything alone," he said.

No one clapped.

That was good.

Sometimes people get applause too early and think they are finished understanding.

They made three trial rules.

On Monday and Thursday, anyone could join.

On Tuesday, there could be a fast game for children who wanted that.

On Wednesday, captains had to choose someone they did not usually choose.

On Friday, the class would ask:

**Did this work, or did it only look kind?**

### 5. The Line Again

The next day, the crack was still in the asphalt.

Samir did not suddenly become Aksel's best friend.

Aksel did not suddenly become a new person.

Maja still waited for Aksel to decide.

Iben did not speak every time.

But something had changed.

When Samir asked to join, Aksel said:

"It is open play today."

It was not the same as an apology.

But it was a door.

Samir walked through it.

Later he kicked the ball over the fence.

Aksel started to say something.

Then he stopped.

Maja laughed a little.

Not at Samir.

Only because the ball had gone so very high.

Samir laughed too.

Iben looked at the crack in the asphalt.

It was still there.

But now children crossed it.

Not all the time.

Not all of them.

But enough for the question to remain alive:

**If a line can move, who is moving it?**

### Reflection Questions

1. Where have you seen an invisible line between people?
2. What is the difference between disagreement and making someone smaller?
3. Why is it not enough to tell everyone to "just be nice"?
4. Who benefits when nobody says anything?
5. What must stop before friendship can begin?
