# Why Can't Everyone Just Be Friends?

## Adult Book: The Room That Paid for Silence

### 1. The Question

Blix asked the question because a child had asked it first.

**Why can't everyone just be friends?**

Adults often smile at that question.

They call it sweet.

They call it innocent.

Sometimes they call it naive so they do not have to answer it.

The Council did not smile.

The Child had brought the question into the room, and The Child was not a decoration.

The Cosmos Minister listened.

Then the room widened.

Schoolyards became feeds.

Feeds became parliaments.

Parliaments became borders.

Borders became graves.

The question remained.

Not smaller.

Sharper.

### 2. The Adult Version

The adult version is not:

Why are people not nicer?

The adult version is:

**What systems reward human beings for making other human beings impossible to hear?**

The Council began there.

Science asked for evidence.

Psychology asked what fear does to groups.

The Power Critic asked who profits from distrust.

The Peacebuilder asked what must stop before dialogue can mean anything.

Christianity asked who had been placed outside the circle of mercy.

Islam asked what justice requires when community has been broken.

Buddhism asked what attachments had become identities.

Plato asked which shadows were being sold as reality.

Kant asked who was being used as a tool for someone else's security, image, or power.

The Child asked:

**If you know this hurts children, why is it still allowed to be called normal?**

No one answered quickly.

That was the first honest act.

### 3. The Elephants

The room had elephants.

Not metaphorical in the soft sense.

Heavy truths taking up space.

One elephant was money.

Conflict is profitable. Fear sells protection, attention, weapons, votes, identity, and belonging.

Another elephant was humiliation.

People rarely become peaceful while being publicly stripped of dignity.

Another was hypocrisy.

Nations condemn violence while preparing for it, sell ideals while trading with cruelty, and speak of human rights as if geography changes the value of a child.

Another was exhaustion.

Many people do not hate their enemies as much as they are too tired to imagine them fully human.

The Cosmos Minister did not remove the elephants.

The Cosmos Minister named them without turning naming into victory.

### 4. Protection Before Peace

The Peacebuilder spoke first.

"Peace cannot mean asking the harmed to remain available to the harmful."

The Council agreed, but not simply.

Protection comes before discussion.

When bombs fall, when children are targeted, when minorities are hunted, when a person is trapped in violence, the first task is not a beautiful conversation.

The first task is to stop harm.

But after protection, the harder question waits.

How do societies stop harm without becoming addicted to enemies?

How do we defend the vulnerable without making vengeance our hidden religion?

How do we remember that accountability is not the same as humiliation?

The claim of truth must never be used as a knife.

But lies must not be allowed to call themselves peace.

### 5. What Friendship Cannot Carry

The word friendship is too small for geopolitics.

It cannot carry borders, resources, history, trauma, propaganda, grief, law, and power.

But it carries one thing adults should not discard:

It refuses to begin with dehumanization.

Friendship, in its deepest form, does not mean agreement.

It means the other person has not been reduced to a category before they speak.

That principle can scale.

Not easily.

Not romantically.

But practically.

Institutions can be built to reduce humiliation.

Media can be designed to slow down rage.

Schools can teach disagreement without contempt.

Leaders can be rewarded for repair, not only domination.

Citizens can refuse the cheap pleasure of making the other side less human.

None of this is enough.

But enough things are made from smaller things that refused to be nothing.

### 6. The Next Work

The Council did not conclude that everyone can simply be friends.

That would be false.

Some people are dangerous.

Some systems must be stopped.

Some harms require law, protection, sanctions, distance, or force restrained by responsibility.

But the Council also refused the opposite lie:

That humanity is doomed to organize itself around enemies.

The Child kept the question alive.

The Cosmos Minister closed only this much:

**Peace begins when protection becomes stronger than revenge, and truth becomes braver than belonging.**

Blix did not receive a final answer.

ReMind does not promise that.

Instead, the room returned the question to us:

**What would we have to stop rewarding if we truly wanted people to stop needing enemies?**

### Reflection Questions

1. What systems profit when people lose trust in each other?
2. Why can protection not be postponed for dialogue?
3. What is the difference between accountability and humiliation?
4. Where do you see dehumanization treated as normal?
5. What would your community have to stop rewarding to become more peaceful?
