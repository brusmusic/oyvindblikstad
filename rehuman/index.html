ReHuman

ReHuman is an immersive sound and vibration system created by Øyvind Blikstad. A space for deep restoration. A return to the body. A quiet reset in a loud world.

Recognition & context

The ReHuman philosophy grows out of years of work with Breathing Cycles — a music-based breathing method used in schools, therapy settings and self-regulation practices in several countries. Featured by NRK and implemented as part of educational materials for emotional awareness and life mastery.

How it works

ReHuman combines low-frequency vibration, spatial sound and guided breathing patterns. The system works directly with the nervous system — slowing the breath, lowering the heart rate and allowing the body to enter a state of deep, natural regulation.

It is non-invasive, non-verbal, non-medical — yet deeply physical. You don’t think your way in. The body remembers.

Research & education

The sound patterns used in ReHuman are based on known physiological principles: vagus nerve stimulation, entrainment, and slow breathing response. These principles are already used in educational and therapeutic contexts, especially within mental health, stress reduction and emotional regulation in schools.
